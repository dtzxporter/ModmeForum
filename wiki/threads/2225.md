<p>Thread By: dr. squidbot<br />Black Ops 3 anims for any previous CoD!<br /><br />(BETA)<br /><br />Now you too can have weird, out of place future weapons in your World War 2 game.<br /><br /><br /><br /><br /><br /><iframe type="text/html" width="640" height="360" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/YhWVhktum60" frameborder="0"></iframe><br /><br /><br /><br />This rig will allow you to easily port animations from Black Ops 3 onto any rig from COD4 to Advanced Warfare.<br /><br /><br /><br />The only thing included is a Maya file, it should be all you need.<br /><br /><br /><br /><img style="max-width: 500px;" src="https://i.gyazo.com/e4fb833498088b3965bc28fc5aa8eb1e.png"><br /><br /><br /><br /><p style="text-align:left;"><strong>NOTICE: There&#39;s sometimes an issue with the bolt and clip transforms resetting to zero. A quick fix for this is to leave the weapon&#39;s bone named as tag_weapon, import the model and move it under the main hierarchy&#39;s tag_weapon, import the animation, and then rename the joint to j_gun &amp; move it to bo2:tag_weapon before export.</strong></p><br /><br /><br /><br /><p style="text-align:left;">All you have to do is set up the rig like you would any other one--drag the j_gun under tag_weapon <strong>in the bo2: namespace </strong>and zero the transforms <strong>(including joint orietnation &amp; rotation)</strong>. After this, you can just import and export anims at your leisure.</p><br /><br /><br /><br /><p style="text-align:left;">When exporting animations &amp; models from Wraith, make sure you have <strong>&quot;Patch model and anim tags to support older games&quot;</strong> <span style="color:#ff3333;"><strong>DISABLED</strong>. </span>Then, just manually rename &quot;tag_weapon&quot; in the viewmodel files to be &quot;j_gun&quot;, easy as that.</p><br /><br /><br /><br /><p style="text-align:left;">When you&#39;re ready to export, select tag_torso and tag_cambone inside of the bo2: namespace, then click Select Hierarchy, and then you can export your xAnim!</p><br /><br /><br /><br /><p style="text-align:left;">Because of a little mistake i made and didn&#39;t catch until I finished the project, ADS anims have to be exported a little differently. Instead of selecting our joints within the bo2 namespace, we&#39;ll select them inside of the standard &quot;Joints&quot; group. Select &quot;tag_view&quot; and &quot;tag_torso&quot;, then export. This should make your ADS anim work fine.</p><br /><br /><br /><br /><p style="text-align:left;">That should be about it! Your T7 anims should now be working on any pair of viewhands. <strong>If you are using this rig, you will have to create a new animmodel. Instead of using Select All and Select Hierarchy, just select the bo2: namespace groups &amp; weapon groups and then select hierarchy. </strong></p><br /><br /><p style="text-align:left;"><strong><span style="color:#ff3333;">IF YOU EXPORT WITH THE STANDARD JOINTS GROUP SELECTED, ASSET MANAGER WILL REJECT YOUR ANIM MODEL.</span></strong></p><br /><br /><br /><br /><p style="text-align:left;">If you use this, I&#39;d appreciate at least a mention in the credits as Dr. LilRobot! Hope you enjoy!! If you have any issues, post them below.</p><br /><br /><br /><br /><p style="text-align:left;"><strong>KNOWN ISSUES:</strong></p><br /><br /><p style="text-align:left;">In very rare cases, the left hand&#39;s fingers will become contorted. This mostly happens on weapons with complicated left-hand poses, i.e. the MX Garand, and is typically not visible to the camera.</p><br /><br /><br /><br /><p style="text-align:left;"><strong>FUTURE PLANS:</strong></p><br /><br /><p style="text-align:left;">Backwards Compatibility to support T5 -&gt; T7 rig conversion</p><br /><br /><br /><br /><br /><br /><br /><br /><span style="color:#ff3333;">DOWNLOAD:</span><br /><br />Requires Maya<br /><br /><a href="https://mega.nz/#!dYsEHBwa!t5ziDCOL69aKBhXecvhjOuMPv6jLUWPFTgfsg13TQz8">Download</a></p>
